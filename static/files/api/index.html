rbastructure module

This preliminary documentation does not contain all elements of the module. However the classes, which are considered most relevant for the user are in this documentation. The NewControler-class is the main-interface and is sufficient for generic predefined workflows. Additionally the SimulationData-class is also relevant for the user, since it provides methods to export the simulation output into various formats. The Problem-class might also be of relevance for more experienced users, who have already an idea how RBA Problems work and have basic knowlege about linear-programming. For those users this class provides methods to make customised manipulations.

Below some simple application workflows, using the api are given as examples. The include: Setting the growth-medium composition and determining the cellular composition at the maximal possible growth rate. Determining the maximum growth rate and cellular compositions for knock-out and wild-type strains. Creating a Pareto-front between the total expression levels of two model-proteins. Determining the minimal and maximal possible concentrations of ribosomes at different growth-rates. Finding the minimally required growth-substrate concentration to allow a given growth-rate and showing the saturation of constraints at this solution. Furthermore in some of the workflows it is exemplified how to record the cellular composition and how to export the data with the SimulationData module. Lastly it is shown how to access information on the model's structure via the api.

Setting medium finding max growth rate and exporting results. 

from rbastructure.NewControler import RBA_newControler
## Load Model ##
pathToModel= "…/…/…" #path to directory with model files#
Simulation = RBA_newControler(pathToModel)

## Set medium to first condition, simulate and record results.##
Simulation.setMedium({'M_glc__D': 10})
Simulation.setMedium({'M_pyr': 0})
Simulation.findMaxGrowthRate()
Simulation.recordResults('Glucose')

## Set medium to second condition, simulate and record results.##
Simulation.setMedium({'M_glc__D': 0})
Simulation.setMedium({'M_pyr': 10})
Simulation.findMaxGrowthRate()
Simulation.recordResults('Pyruvate')

## Write recorded results to RBA_SimulationData object ##
Simulation.writeResults(session_name='GlcPyrTest')

## Export results in various formats ##
Simulation.SimulationData.exportCSV()
Simulation.SimulationData.exportEscherMap(type='investment')
Simulation.SimulationData.exportEscherMap(type='fluxes')
Simulation.SimulationData.exportProteoMap()

Determining the pareto-optimal relationship between the total expression of two model-proteins at different growth-rates. 


from rbastructure.NewControler import RBA_newControler
## Load Model ##
pathToModel= "…/…/…" #path to directory with model files#
Simulation = RBA_newControler(pathToModel)

## Add two proteins to problem ##
Simulation.addProtein('Protein1')
Simulation.addProtein('Protein2')
## Determine maximum growth rate ##
Mumax=Simulation.findMaxGrowthRate()
## determine pareto fron between total expression levels of two proteins ##
## at 90 percent of the maximum growth rate ##
Simulation.setMu(Mumax*0.9)
PF=Simulation.ParetoFront(['TotalLevel_Protein1','TotalLevel_Protein2'], 20)
## Write Paretofront to csv ##
PF.to_csv('Pareto_Protein1_Protein2_90maxMu.csv')
## determine pareto fron between total expression levels of two proteins ##
## at 99 percent of the maximum growth rate ##
Simulation.setMu(Mumax*0.99)
PF=Simulation.ParetoFront(['TotalLevel_Protein1','TotalLevel_Protein2'], 20)
## Write Paretofront to csv ##
PF.to_csv('Pareto_Protein1_Protein2_99maxMu.csv')

Determining the feasible range of ribosome concentration at different growth-rates. 


from rbastructure.NewControler import RBA_newControler
## Load Model ##
pathToModel= "…/…/…" #path to directory with model files#
Simulation = RBA_newControler(pathToModel)

## Determine maximum growth rate ##
Mumax=Simulation.findMaxGrowthRate()

## Determin feasible range (variability) of ribosome concentration ##
## at 90 percent of the maximum growth rate and print range##

FR_90=Simulation.FeasibleRage('RibosomeID')
print(FR_90)

## at 99 percent of the maximum growth rate and print range ##

FR_99=Simulation.FeasibleRage('RibosomeID')
print(FR_99)

Determine minimum required glucose level in aerobic conditions at 90 percent of maxim growth rate. And subsequently determine saturation-level of constraints at solution. Furthermore one reaction is constrained to work in forward direction 


from rbastructure.NewControler import RBA_newControler
## Load Model ##
pathToModel= "…/…/…" #path to directory with model files#
Simulation = RBA_newControler(pathToModel)


## Force ReactionXY to carry positive flux ##
Simulation.Problem.setLB({'ReactionXY':0})

## Determine maximum growth rate ##
Mumax=Simulation.findMaxGrowthRate()

## Find and print the minimal concentration of glucose in the medium, ##
## which is required to sustain 90 percent of the maximal growth rate ##
## in aerobic condiditon.
Simulation.setMu(Mumax*0.9)
Simulation.setMedium({'M_o2': 10})
minGlc=Simulation.findMinMediumConcentration('M_glc__D')
print(minGlc)

## Determine and print saturation-level for all constraints
SaturationDict=ConstraintSaturation()
print({c: SaturationDict[c]['LHS']/SaturationDict[c]['RHS'] for c in list(SaturationDict.keys())})
